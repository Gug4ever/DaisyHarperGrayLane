Her Story

The life of the last pharaoh of Egypt.

69 BCE

A Royal Birth

Cleopatra VII was born in Alexandria into the Ptolemaic dynasty. Her name comes from the Greek words for "glory" and "father." Though her family was Greek, she became the first Ptolemy to learn and speak Egyptian fluently.

Early Years

A Brilliant Mind

Raised in Alexandria, Cleopatra received an elite education in mathematics, philosophy, astronomy, and public speaking. She spoke at least nine languages, possibly a dozen, including Greek, Egyptian, and Latin.

55 BCE

Co-Ruler at 14

Her father, Pharaoh Ptolemy XII, made her co-ruler of Egypt when she was just fourteen. She ruled alongside him for four years, gaining invaluable political experience.

51 BCE

Queen of Egypt

After her father's death, Cleopatra ascended the throne at 18. She initially ruled with her younger brother Ptolemy XIII, but tensions quickly escalated into a civil war for control of the throne.

48 BCE

Meeting Julius Caesar

Forced into exile, Cleopatra famously had herself smuggled into Caesar's quarters wrapped in a carpet. She charmed him, and with his military support, she regained control of Egypt.

44–31 BCE

Alliance with Mark Antony

After Caesar's assassination, Cleopatra formed a powerful alliance with Roman general Mark Antony. Together they had three children and challenged Octavian for control of the Roman world.

31 BCE

The Battle of Actium

Cleopatra personally commanded Egyptian warships in the naval battle against Octavian. Defeated, she and Antony retreated to Alexandria. Their defeat marked the end of an era.

30 BCE

The Last Pharaoh

Cleopatra was the last pharaoh of Egypt. After her death, the country became a province of the Roman Empire. Her tomb has never been found, and archaeologists continue to search for it to this day.

Though her reign ended in tragedy, Cleopatra's legacy endures. She is remembered as a brilliant and powerful ruler who navigated the complex world of Roman politics to protect her kingdom.
